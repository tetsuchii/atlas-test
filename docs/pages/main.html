HOW MIGHT WE STAVE OFF THE END OF THE WORLD?

HOW IS YOUR DESIGN THINKING WORKING OUT SO FAR?

As designers, we just spent the past 25 years talking about saving the planet, transitioning to new and more equitable businesses, being the force of good, being "human-centric" (or whatever is the BS young people say these days). But the world is still in flames, there is war, exploitation, plastic waste, unbreathable air… you know, that thing we are trying to call by the pet name “permacrisis”. We as designers were complacent in the creation of a well crafted extinction event. Yay us.

How the f*ck did that happen? Are we THAT stupid? Or are we just THAT suicidal?

READ MORE

Maybe design could finally grow up if we stopped pretending the world is a tidy user-journey. Reality is hyperreal, mediated, glitching at the seams—and our work should meet that complexity head-on instead of dieting on 2000s-era optimism.

MOVE BEYOND HUMAN-CENTRIC IDEAS

Move beyond the fantasy of the “average user.” Design for messy, mediated realities—where algorithms, objects, places, and power shape experience as much as people ever did.

LET GO OF GENERIC METHODOLOGIES

Let go of templated toolkits and innovation theatre. Work with the world as it actually is: contradictory, asymmetrical, political—and demanding design that’s more than process cosplay.
