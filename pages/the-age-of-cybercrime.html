Mastercard invited us to create a thought-leadership whitepaper that reframes cybercrime as a systemic, ecosystem-level challenge rather than a purely technical issue. We conducted extensive research combining Mastercard’s Cyber Insights data, market analysis, expert interviews, and a population-level digital security survey to understand how cybercrime evolves across tactics, techniques, and procedures.

Click to see images

Our team synthesized global threat trends with the everyday realities of users and service providers, revealing how automation, AI, and criminal collaboration reshape the cybersecurity landscape. We translated these findings into a clear narrative structure supported by actionable frameworks bridging the gap between professionals and business decisionmakers through a common TTP model to help organizations see where their responsibilities lie and how their decisions affect the broader digital ecosystem.

The whitepaper was designed as a public resource: accessible enough for consumers and SMEs, yet strategic enough for regulators, financial institutions, and enterprise leaders. It became a core piece of Mastercard’s communication, workshop materials, and educational outreach across the region. You can also read it here: Click to read more!

OUR INVOLVEMENT

  • Full research and synthesis of threat intelligence, secondary analysis and survey data
  • Expert interviews and qualitative insight development
  • Framework creation (TTP model adaptation for public use)
  • Narrative design, concept development, and content writing
  • Visual narrative and structure for public dissemination

OUTCOME

The whitepaper became a widely used communication and workshop asset, strengthening Mastercard’s position as a trusted thought leader and providing stakeholders with clear, actionable guidance for understanding and responding to cyber threats.

IMPACT

By clarifying the shared responsibilities across users, businesses, and public institutions, the material supports more resilient digital behavior, fosters cross-sector collaboration, and encourages the adoption of safer, more secure digital practices across society.
