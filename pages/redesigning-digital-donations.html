SOS Children’s Villages invited us to conduct an in-depth research project to better understand and improve the donor experience across both online and offline touchpoints. The goal was to capture the motivations, expectations, and behaviours of donors, while also identifying global trends shaping the future of digital giving.

Click to see images

Through qualitative interviews, field observations of on-street fundraising, and an extensive desktop trend review, we examined how trust is formed, what drives donors to commit, and how technology and communication influence the giving journey. Our work covered the full donor lifecycle—from awareness to long-term engagement—revealing where friction, confusion, or missed opportunities appeared.

The insights allowed us to highlight actionable directions for improving messaging, strengthening donor relationships, and preparing the organisation for modern, mobile-first donation flows. The research now serves as a strategic foundation for SOS as they evolve their digital fundraising efforts.

OUR INVOLVEMENT

  • Qualitative interviews with donors
  • UX design for the chartplotter and vessel monitoring system
  • Trend and best-practice research
  • Observation of on-street fundraising processes
  • Donor journey and experience analysis
  • Development of improvement directions and recommendations

OUTCOME

The research clarified where donor trust and engagement can be strengthened, and revealed opportunities to simplify digital donation flows. The organisation gained a clearer, more structured understanding of how to modernise the donor experience in line with international best practices.

IMPACT

The recommendations support long-term donor growth, build trust in the nonprofit sector, and help ensure more children receive stable and sustainable support.
