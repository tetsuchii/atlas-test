BKK asked us to examine how travellers encounter and engage with Budapest Pay&GO across the ecosystem of screens, signs, and street-level interactions. We approached the service as a complex system rather than a set of isolated interfaces, mapping how digital design, physical touchpoints and user expectations shape one another in real use.

Click to see images

Over five days of field research, we observed and intercepted hundreds of riders—mostly visitors navigating an unfamiliar city—capturing the subtle behaviours and misunderstandings that don’t appear in controlled environments. In parallel, we conducted a detailed audit of the Pay&GO digital experience: websites, validators, the information architecture behind the service, and the customer portal where trust is either reinforced or lost.

We also analysed communication materials, naming conventions, and brand cues to understand how the service is framed in the minds of users. By combining these layers—heuristics, analytics, field insight, and usability testing—we produced a clear set of directions to make Pay&GO more intuitive, legible, and aligned with how people move through complex urban systems.

OUR INVOLVEMENT

  • UX and IA evaluation of the Pay&GO digital ecosystem
  • Analytics review of user paths and discovery patterns
  • Field observation and on-site intercept interviews
  • Usability testing of purchase and verification flows
  • Service, UX, and communication improvement guidelines

OUTCOME

We delivered a consolidated insight base and focused UX recommendations that strengthened clarity, reduced friction, and supported BKK in improving Pay&GO’s overall service experience.

IMPACT

A clearer, more comprehensible Pay&GO system encourages wider adoption of fast, paperless public transport options and builds long-term trust in digital mobility services.
