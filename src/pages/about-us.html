"DESIGN INNOVATION"

THAT FREAKING VENN DIAGRAM

If that diagram made any sense, we would consider heroin the perfect product. It's quite cheap to make ("viable"), easy, too ("feasible"), and oh, how desirable... Just being desirable doesn't make a product or service good, or human-centric, it only makes it sellable. And just because it's feasible to do something, doesn't mean it should be done. And certainly doesn't make it innovative. Oversimplificationmuch?

First runner up

"DESIGN MUST BE HUMAN-CENTRIC"

That's mighty Kantian, but why exactly? Just because we understand the needs of humans (actually, it's usually customers/users), doesn't mean we can design great stuff. Design is an intervention in complicated ecosystems that include tons of elements, some are human, some are non-human, some natural, some artificial... Understanding humans is about 3% of what's needed for great design. There is still that 83% for ecosystem understanding. The rest is just dumb luck.

Honorable mention

"DESIGN IS A METHODOLOGY"

No it's not. And the more we say it, the more we reduce designers to "process operators" - interchangable followers of pre-written playbooks and method kits promising that whoever uses them will get great and innovative results. You don't need designers for that - even ChatGPT can do that (yes, we tried it, not as bad as you'd think).
